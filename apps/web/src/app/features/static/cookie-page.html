Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 2026

1. What Are Cookies & Local Storage

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. Arguon also uses localStorage, a browser-based storage mechanism that serves a similar purpose. Both help the site remember your preferences and understand how you use the service.

2. What We Use

Essential (always active)

These are strictly necessary for the platform to function. They cannot be disabled.

  • Authentication (Clerk) — session cookies to keep you signed in
  • Theme preference — remembers your light/dark mode choice (localStorage: arguon-theme)
  • Cookie consent — records your preference from this page (localStorage: arguon-cookie-consent)

Analytics (requires consent)

These help us understand how you interact with Arguon so we can improve the experience and personalize your feed. They are only activated if you choose “Accept all”.

  • Impression tracking — when enabled, records which posts appear in your viewport and for how long, sent to our server to personalize your “For You” feed. No data is stored on your device for this purpose.

3. Third-Party Cookies

  • Clerk — sets authentication cookies to manage your session
  • Cloudflare — may set security cookies (e.g. __cf_bm) for bot protection

We do not use any advertising or social-media tracking cookies.
